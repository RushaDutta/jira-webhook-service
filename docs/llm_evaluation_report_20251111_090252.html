Despite added login friction, compliance needs and brand trust override user experience concerns. The security team's bias toward risk reduction aligns with enterprise security standards, making this the highest priority.Reduced unauthorized login attempts by 68%, improving platform security and trust.https://lookerstudio.google.com/reporting/0dcc27d8-13a8-4025-b9dd-a09e1dd78bf1SCRUM-25's rationale emphasized compliance and brand trust, yet the 68% security improvement impact doesn't quantify compliance adherence or trust metrics. SCRUM-26Single Sign-On (SSO)8SSO addresses enterprise client demands, which drives sales and contract renewals. While setup complexity and IT dependency are drawbacks, the feature's alignment with enterprise growth and sales incentives elevates its priority. However, its optional adoption by smaller users reduces its universal urgency.Increased enterprise trial conversions and reduced password reset requests, proving high business impact.https://lookerstudio.google.com/reporting/0dcc27d8-13a8-4025-b9dd-a09e1dd78bf1The rationale emphasized enterprise sales alignment but overlooked that reduced password resets—a universal benefit not tied to STAR Priority—was a key impact. Learnings: balance sales-driven priorities with cross-user benefits and verify assumed adoption rates among smaller segments to avoid overestimating feature urgency.
